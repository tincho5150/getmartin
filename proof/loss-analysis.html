ProvisionAI's buyers were convinced their supply chains were fine. No pain, no urgency, no reason to change. The product was excellent. The prospect couldn't feel why they needed it. So we built a tool that made them feel it — before a single sales call.

Supply Chain Loss Analysis
The core insight
Buyers don't resist buying — they resist feeling the problem. A tool that surfaces hidden cost in their own words removes the resistance before the sales conversation begins.

Module 1 — Load optimisation

Score yourself.
Honestly.

How the diagnostic works

Four modules. Each one a domain where supply chains lose money invisibly — load optimisation, network flow, OTIF performance, system synchronisation. The prospect rates themselves 1, 2, or 3 on each statement. No benchmarks shown until they've scored themselves.

Why this order matters

The tool shows the standard first — what good looks like. Then the prospect assesses their own gap against it. They define the problem themselves. The tool just holds the mirror.

Diagnostic complete — 67% gap

Synthesis — personalised output

Their roadmap,
built from their own scores.

What the roadmap does

Once all four modules are complete, the tool ranks the gaps. The lowest score becomes priority #1 automatically. The prospect sees a five-step fix for their most critical gap — generated from the answers they just gave, not from a generic playbook.

Why personalisation changes the conversation

A generic recommendation is easy to dismiss. A recommendation that traces directly back to their own scores is much harder to argue with. They built the brief. The tool built the plan.

Prioritised action plan

Module 3 — OTIF performance

Eight levers.
Most teams pull two.

What this module reveals

OTIF failures start upstream in planning — not on the dock or the road. The tool shows all eight levers that control on-time delivery. Most operations are using two or three. The other five are active sources of failure hiding in plain sight.

The Ask Provision feature

An AI assistant embedded in the tool answers questions about scores, levers, and where to start. The prospect can ask in plain language — "what is OTIF?" or "where do I start?" — and get a direct answer without leaving the diagnostic.

OTIF performance — 8 levers

Module 1 — The playbook

Not just the gap.
The five-step fix.

What the playbook delivers

Each module has three tabs — the standard, the self-assessment, and the playbook. The playbook isn't generic advice. It's a sequenced five-step fix for the specific gap the prospect just identified. Item master accuracy. Math-based load building. Dock-level loading diagrams. Each step closes a defined percentage of the gap.

What this means for the sales call

The prospect arrives with a gap score, a ranked priority list, and a five-step fix. The rep's job is to show how the product executes the playbook — not to convince the prospect they have a problem.

What changes in the room

Before this tool, every discovery call started cold — the rep had to establish that a problem existed before they could talk about the solution. Now the prospect arrives knowing their gap score, their #1 priority, and the five steps to fix it. The call starts at step three of a conversation that used to take three calls to reach.
